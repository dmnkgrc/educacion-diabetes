  • Introduction

    When the basal rate is set, your insulin pump delivers basal insulin every few minutes, 24 hours a day. This flow of insulin is called the basal rate. Your basal rate is calculated by your doctor or healthcare team to meet your insulin needs. The Basal Rate Profile of your insulin pump consists of up to 24 different hourly basal rates. Each hourly rate may be changed independently. In this lesson, we focus on how to program your Basal Rate Profile on the insulin pump. Before you start, make sure you have your personal values for basal rate settings from your doctor or healthcare team.

  • Goals

    At the end of this lesson, you will know how to:

    • Program your insulin Basal Rate Profile on the insulin pump
    • Copy your hourly Basal Rate Profile
    • Check that your Basal Rate Profile is stored on the insulin pump

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 1

    Your insulin pump offers 5 different Basal Rate Profile options. In the STANDARD user menu there is one Basal Rate Profile available. Find out how to access the other four in the Advanced Training Module. Press to move to the BASAL RATE PROGRAMMING 1 screen.

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 2

    Press to select. The daily BASAL RATE TOTAL is displayed.

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 3

    Press to move to the first hour. This is always 00:00 - 01:00 (12 AM - 01 AM if the 12h (American) time format is used).

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 4

    Press or or scroll to set the hourly basal rate for each hour given by your doctor or healthcare team. The increments shown depend on the programmed basal rate: 0.01 U/h from 0.05 U/h to 1.00 U/h, 0.05 U/h from 1.00 U/h to 10.0 U/h, and 0.1 U/h from 10.0 U/h to 50.0 U/h.

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 5

    Press to move to the next hour.

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 6

    Continue using and or to set the hourly basal rates for the remaining hours. This allows you to program your individual Basal Rate Profile on an hour-by-hour basis. Continue until all 24 hours are programmed.

    Next lesson
    Connecting cartridge, adapter and infusion set

  • Programming your Basal Rate Profile: step 7

    Press to show the total BASAL RATE. The new daily BASAL RATE TOTAL is displayed. Check if the total corresponds with the total you got from your healthcare team. If the total is not correct, then check each hour by pressing the menu key and re-enter the right amount where needed. If the total is correct, press to save the profile and press to exit.

    Next lesson

  • Copying an hourly basal rate: step 1

    If your basal rate is the same for multiple hours in a row, you can save time by copying an hourly basal rate to one or more hourly timeframes. Here is how.

    Press to move to the BASAL RATE PROGRAMMING 1 screen.

    Press to select. The daily BASAL RATE TOTAL is displayed.

    Press to move to the hourly basal rate you want to copy.

  • Copying an hourly basal rate: step 4

    Press and simultaneously. A special beep confirms that you are pressing the keys correctly. The same hourly basal rate is copied to the next hour.

    If you want to copy the basal rate to multiple hours, press and repeatedly, or press and hold and (scroll).

    Press to confirm. Check the new daily BASAL RATE TOTAL.

    Press to save and exit.

  • How to check your Basal Rate Profile is stored

    To check that your Basal Rate Profile is stored on the pump, enter the BASAL RATE PROGRAMMING 1 screen on the pump. Press to display the Basal Rate Total screen. If you saved your profile, you'll see the amount of insulin you entered on the screen (the number of insulin units). If the number of insulin units reads 0, then your Basal Rate Profile is not programmed and you will need to return to Step 1.
